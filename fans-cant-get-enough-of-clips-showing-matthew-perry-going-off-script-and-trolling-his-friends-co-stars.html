Matthew Perry Loved Going Off Script And Trolling His Fellow Cast Members On Friends

Throughout his journey on Friends, Matthew Perry felt at home alongside his castmates. The late actor did admit to struggling however, always wanting a laugh out of audience. Nonetheless, his contributions won't be forgotten as Perry put a smile on the face of millions, and that held true for his castmates as well.

As we're going to show in the video below posted to X, Perry never took himself too seriously, and he wasn't afraid to waste a take either. If a cast member made a mistake, Perry would be sure to on top of it in the following scene.

There were so many examples, like the time Courteney Cox accidentally looked right into the camera. Perry would troll Cox in the following scene, looking directly into the camera, saying his line, "I know, what are we going to do."

Without a doubt, Matthew Perry made the experience on Friends a better one for everyone around him.

The Entire Cast Of Friends Loved Going Off Script To Make A Scene Better

Not only did Matthew Perry go off-script, like the time he trolled Matt LeBlanc with the Q-tip joke, but the same held true for the rest of the cast throughout the series. David Schwimmer's iconic "I take thee Rachel" line was actually unscripted, and inspired by a moment that took place off-camera.

Other iconic scenes were also improvised by the cast. One of them included Rachel using, "this is the world's worst hangover" line, which received a huge reaction from the audience, so much so that Friends had to edit the crowd noise after the shoot took place.

In truth, that's exactly what made the cast so special. They weren't afraid to have fun, and they always wanted what was best for the show. They would typically sitdown and watch episodes together. In that moment, the cast would always give each other tips on how to get better.

The unity between the main six was very apparent, and it would lead to huge success during its decade long run.

Former Co-Stars Of Matthew Perry Are Paying Their Respects Following His Passing

“We are devastated to learn of Matthew Perry’s passing. He was a true gift to us all. Our heart goes out to his family, loved ones, and all of his fans.” Those were the words written by the Friends account over on Twitter. As expected, tributes were piling in following the announcement of Matthew Perry's death.

Maggie Wheeler was another castmate that worked close to Perry throughout her experience on the show. As expected, the loss was a heartbreaking one for the actress.

She wrote on Instagram, “What a loss. The world will miss you Mathew Perry (sic). The joy you brought to so many in your too short lifetime will live on. I feel so very blessed by every creative moment we shared.”

Without a doubt, the tributes will only continue in the upcoming days. One can only imagine what Perry's family is going through at this time, and the same holds true for his fellow cast members Matt LeBlanc, Jennifer Aniston, David Schwimmer, Lisa Kudrow and Courteney Cox.

Although he'll always be remembered for his time on the show as Chandler, the actor wanted to be remembered more for his contributions with others, especially those that are battling addiction issues. Thanks to his book and interviews, Perry was able to spread awareness before his passing.
