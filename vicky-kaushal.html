Vicky Kaushal is an Indian actor who has risen to prominence in the last few years with his stellar performances in films like Masaan (2015), Uri: The Surgical Strike (2019), and Raazi (2018). He won a National Award for Uri: The Surgical Strike in 2019 and has also received several awards such as “Best Male Debut” for Masaan and “Best Supporting Actor” for the Sanjay Dutt biopic Sanju (2018). His physical transformation in Uri helped cement his place in Bollywood as one of the best actors from the younger generation. Though not considered conventionally handsome, Vicky’s rugged looks and charming smile has earned him a huge female following. He has more than 200k followers on Facebook, over 300k followers on Twitter, and more than 4 million followers on Instagram.

    Personal Trainer

    Vicky gained 15 kgs of muscle to play an army officer in the military action film Uri: The Surgical Strike. According to his fitness trainer Rakesh Udiyar, his transformation was a two-pronged process and included a highly-regimented workout routine and closely supervised diet plan. As Vicky has an ectomorphic body type, characterized by a fast metabolism and difficulty in gaining weight, his trainer prepared his diet plan to include a lot of carbs and proteins.

    His diet plan was constructed in 3 different phases to achieve the desired body –

    • First Phase – The actor was put on a 6-meal-a-day plan, which meant he had to eat regularly at an interval of 3 hours. His calorie consumption was set to 2500, with more focus on carbohydrates and proteins, and carbs like oats and brown rice were included only in the first 3 meals of the day. Protein-rich foods like chicken (200 grams), fish, mutton, and eggs (8 eggs) became his staple diet in the first phase. He also supplemented it with vegetables, smoothies, and juices.
    • Second Phase – In order to gain muscle, and not fat, Vicky was put on a ketogenic diet. Before working out, he would drink an 800-calorie smoothie of bananas, yogurt, and berries, twice a day, to rid his body of water retention from the earlier phase. Vicky consumed 3400 Calories daily and at the end of his keto diet, had put on a significant amount of weight.
    • Third Phase – In the last phase, his diet was switched back to include lots of carbs and proteins. His daily consumption was set to 2000 calories.

    In preparation for his role, Vicky received boot-camp training for 2 months following which, he trained in mixed martial arts and military training for 5 months each. He would start his day at 6:30 am and hit the gym for 2 hours doing weight lifting exercises. It would be followed by an hour-long session in mixed martial arts in the afternoon. He would end his days by doing military training for 3 hours from 7 pm to 10 pm under a Sikh regiment at the Cuffe Parade in Mumbai. To make his muscles look more prominent, Vicky underwent the ‘Time Under Tension’ training in which he would lift heavy weights with 8 to 12 repetitions in each set to cause his muscles to grow faster (known as hypertrophy).

    Vicky Kaushal Facts

  • He grew up in an impoverished household and aspired to have a stable career after completing his studies. He landed a job in an IT company but decided to tear the offer letter in order to follow his true passion, acting.
  • His family roots are in Hoshiarpur in Punjab.
  • Some of his hobbies are reading, traveling, gymming, and dancing.
  • Before finding success as an actor, he assisted director Anurag Kashyap on his 2012 crime-drama film, Gangs of Wasseypur.
  • After starring in back-to-back hit films in 2018, Vicky hiked up his fees to Rs. 3 crores per film.
  • In 2018, Kaushal was enlisted in the ’30 Under 30′ list by Forbes India.
  • Vicky is a skilled dancer.
  • He has hydrophobia and as such, does not know swimming.
  • He and his brother Sunny were both asked to audition for the sports film Gold (2018) starring Akshay Kumar. However, as Vicky was busy with the shoot of Sanju, only Sunny ended up auditioning and bagged the role of hockey player Himmat Singh.
  • His first leading role was in the hugely successful film Uri: The Surgical Strike. As of 2019, the film became the 10th highest-grossing film ever in terms of domestic collections.
  • Vicky met with an accident on the set of the horror film Bhoot – Part One: The Haunted Ship. Unfortunately, he fractured his cheekbone while shooting an action sequence and had to get 13 stitches.
  • He auditioned for the role of Deepika Padukone‘s husband, Raja Rawal Ratan Singh, in the period drama Padmaavat (2018). However, it was reported that Vicky got rejected in favor of a well-known actor like Shahid Kapoor on the insistence of Deepika, who was not comfortable shooting intimate scenes with a newcomer.
  • In August 2019, Vicky won the National Award for ‘Best Actor’ for his work in Uri: The Surgical Strike. He shared the award with fellow actor and friend Ayushmann Khurrana, who received the prestigious award for Andhadhun (2018).
  • In April 2021, it was revealed that he had been tested positive for COVID-19.
  • In November 2021, Vicky had rented an apartment in Juhu for 60 months for 8 lakhs per month for the initial 36 months. For the next 12 months, it was 8.4 lakhs per month and for the last 12 months, it was fixed at 8.82 lakhs per month. He had made a security deposit of 1.75 crores INR.
  • Vicky Kaushal won the Best Actor title at the IIFA Awards 2022 for his appearance in Sardar Udham.
  • In July 2022, Vicky Kaushal and Katrina Kaif had received death threats from a person named Manvinder Singh alias Aditya Rajput on social media. Hence, a police case was registered at the Santacruz Police Station and he was arrested thereafter.
  • During the 67th Filmfare Awards held in 2022, Vicky Kaushal won the Best Actor (Critics) award for the film Sardar Udham.
  • Sardar Udham was Vicky’s 1st biopic. His second biopic was Sam Bahadur (in which he played the role of Sam Manek Shaw). His 3rd biopic was Dhyan Chand.
  • Vicky was not the first choice to play Dhyan Chand, the film was first offered to Shah Rukh Khan (since he had already played a hockey coach in Chak De India). After his rejection, it went to Ranbir Kapoor (he didn’t want to play any sports biopic other than football), and then to Varun Dhawan (who was already working on the Arun Khetarpal biopic).
  • In December 2023, Vicky became the first Indian actor to get followed by Instagram’s official handle (or page).
  • After the success of Sam Bahadur, he started charging ₹20 crore per film.
