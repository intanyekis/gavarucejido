Harry Bush Obituary, Naples, FL Esteemed Resident Has Sadly Passed Away, Funeral Details

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, August 23, 2024
0SHARESShareTweet

Harry Bush Obituary, Death – Harry David Bush, a beloved figure in Naples, FL, passed away on February 6, 2024, at the age of 93. Born in Tanner, WV, on December 21, 1930, to Carl and Gertrude Black Bush, Harry’s life was characterized by a deep dedication to education, sports, and the development of young minds. His legacy as an educator and coach spanned 34 years, leaving an indelible mark on the lives of those he touched.

A Devoted Marriage and Family Man

On April 14, 1952, in Oakland, MD, Harry married the love of his life, Christine Hyer. Their union lasted for an impressive 71 years, a testament to the strength of their bond and commitment to each other. Together, they created a family grounded in love, with daughters Linda Deaton and Rebecca Furbee. Harry’s role as a father and grandfather, affectionately known as “Papaw,” was marked by warmth, guidance, and a profound love for his family.

An Accomplished Athlete and Educator

Harry’s journey in education and sports began with his graduation from Tanner HS. He continued his academic pursuits at Alderson Broaddus College, earning a BA, and later achieved his MA at West Virginia University. Inducted into the Alderson Broaddus Battler Hall of Fame in 1997, Harry was recognized for his achievements as a two-sport athlete, showcasing his prowess on the field and court. His career as a basketball, baseball, and football coach spanned various high schools, including Stanley HS, VA, Tanner HS, and Glenville HS, WV, as well as River High School, Hannibal, OH.

A Multifaceted Individual

Beyond his contributions to education and sports, Harry was a multifaceted individual with diverse interests. An independent oil and gas developer, he displayed a remarkable ability to balance his professional pursuits. Widely read and known for his genuine interest in others, Harry mentored numerous young adults, leaving a lasting impact on their lives. His engaging personality allowed him to connect with people from all walks of life, making him a cherished friend to many.

Recognition for Distinguished Service

In 2019, the River HS Alumni Association honored Harry with The Harry E. Mallett Distinguished Service Award, acknowledging his exceptional contributions to the school community. This recognition underscored the profound impact he had on the lives of students and colleagues alike.

A Great Girl Dad and Papaw

Harry’s role as a father and grandfather held special significance in his life. Described as a “great girl Dad and Papaw,” he shared his love for life, sports, and learning with his family. The cherished moments spent together created a bond that will be forever treasured by Linda, Rebecca, and their families.

A Man of Connection and Conversation

Known for his ability to engage in meaningful conversations, Harry’s sociable nature endeared him to many. His golfing buddies were among his close companions, and his willingness to connect with anyone made him a respected figure within the community. Harry’s genuine interest in people’s lives and stories contributed to the warmth and camaraderie he shared with others.

Expressing Gratitude and Giving Back

In expressing gratitude for the kindness of his Foxfire neighbors and AVOW Hospice, Harry’s family acknowledges the support that surrounded him during his final days. In lieu of flowers, the family encourages donations to organizations close to Harry’s heart, including the Tanner High School Alumni Association and Tanner Chapel.

These contributions serve as a fitting tribute to a man who dedicated his life to education, sportsmanship, and the well-being of his community. Harry David Bush’s legacy lives on through the lives he touched, the minds he shaped, and the love he shared with family and friends. As the Naples community mourns his passing, his spirit endures in the memories of those who were fortunate enough to know him.
