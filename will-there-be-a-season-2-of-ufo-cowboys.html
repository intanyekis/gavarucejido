Yes, there will be a season 2 of UFO Cowboys on Roku Channel. UFO Cowboys season 2 release date is in 2024.

The TV series follows a group of ranchers called Cowboy Sky Watchers, who joined the mission to explore paranormal adventures along the 37th Parallel.

They will examine the supernatural activities throughout the American West area of UFO Superhighway.

The location is mythical for various UFO sightings alongside more paranormal and extraterrestrial sightings than anywhere else in the USA.

The ranchers will use ancient tracking approaches and modern technology to analyze mysterious activity in the ranches and farms of the American West.

Will There Be A Season 2 Of UFO Cowboys?

Yes, there will be a season 2 of UFO Cowboys. UFO Cowboys Season 2 release date is in 2024 and the first season is available to watch on Roku Channel.

The first season started filming in July 2022, and UFO Cowboys Roku cast Josh Tyler announced the news about a new project in January 2023.

Season 1 of UFO Cowboys was released on July 18, 2023, on Roku Channel, and the show received positive feedback from the viewers.

The audience loved the series and expecting the new season next year.

However, the production team and the cast members have not made an official announcement for its second season yet.

Analyzing the cast members’ remarks, they seemed to be working on the 2nd season.

Even Parker UFO Cowboys pop, Ron Ellis, commented he is looking for season 2. His friends in Callie say the 2nd season needs eight more episodes.

Paker thanked his father and felt glad his dad’s friends liked it.

So, the Cowboy Sky Watchers may return with a new adventure showing paranormal activities in the American West area in late 2024.

Meet The Cast Of UFO Cowboys On Roku

UFO Cowboys Roku cast consits of Ora Brown, Parker Howell, Jeff Sailors, Hadley Sanders, Kaleb Summers, and Josh Tyler.

Ora Brown

Ora Brown is a SAG-AFTRA stuntman, entrepreneur, and actor.

He previously worked in the show The Harder They Fall as a stunt rider. He will appear in upcoming shows like King Ivory and Horizon: An American Saga 2.

Also, he is the CEO of Orí Cowboy Clothing Co.

Since childhood, he shared a dream to start his clothing company.

The entrepreneur’s father inspired him to follow the Western culture and cowboy life when he was only 6. His dad was a working ranch cowboy who used to share his old cowboy stories.

Blending his passion and giving tribute to his dad, he opened his brand, Ori.

He has been married to Blakelea Brown for eight years now. The couple has two sons.

Parker Howell

Parker is one of the ranchers and trackers in the group of Cowboy Sky Watchers.

According to his social profile, he kept himself busy on the rodeo trail or out in the woods, when he was not with the Cowboy Skywatchers.

Jeff Sailors

Jeff Sailors is an actor, storyteller, and adventurer. He has spent pert-near fifty years packing horses and mules in the wilderness.

He is also a Master Horse Hair Hitcher. His works have been featured in museums, private collections, and coffee table books.

Hadley Sanders

Hadley Sanders is a Cowboy at Rodeo. He is from Franklin, Georgia.

Previously, he worked at Sanders Livestock.

Recently, he was living with his partner Aggie Banquet in Miami, Oklahoma.

Kaleb Summers

Kaleb Summers is a TV personality, PRCA Steer Wrestler, and a Youtuber from Claremore, Oklahoma.

Besides this, he is a professional cowboy and adventure enthusiast. He is also a refined expert in the realms of noodling and pursuit of big game hunting.

He completed his graduation from Oklahoma State University in 2011.

Josh Tyler

Josh Tyler is a former pro fighter, sportsperson, and part-time adventurer.

Tyler is the co-founder of Savage Gentleman.

Besides this, he is also a professional Mixed Martial Arts Fighter out of Jeremy Horn’s Elite Performance Gym.

In 2018, he participated in the Ultimate Ninja Challenge Dig Deep to Survive on Discovery.

He married Sarah Tyler, and the couple has three children, Jedidiah Aaron Tyler, Judaha Braham, and Maiverly.

Is UFO Cowboys Real Or Fake?

UFO Cowboys is fake, per fans who watched all eight episodes of the new show.

After watching all eight episodes, viewers found it mildly amusing. They felt it was the best Hollywood hoax since the Blair Witch Project.

However, from the filmography, they learned the reality show produced is actually fake. Even they are sad that UFO Cowboys is staged.

The scene where Jeff gets kidnapped by the government in a white room makes them question more about its originality.

In the room, he had no film crew or friends with him to video his abduction, but still, they displayed it on the screen.

They feel it was high when the guy went missing in the light, reappeared, and attacked them without wearing clothes.

So, they believed it was the same hoax reality docu-drama as the “Blair Witch Project” and others.

While some pointed out it’s the inferior version of ‘The Secret of Skinwalker Ranch.’

Some say the first episode starts as reasonable, but they didn’t hold back the nonstop action. They had fans until the last two minutes of episode 1.

Nonetheless, viewers believed the production team had done their research, studied every UFO trope, and then made a reality show that touched on all of them. So, it is a fun ride for UFO lovers.

Some even appreciate the acknowledgment of extraterrestrials but advised the production team to put a considerable disclaimer on each episode acknowledging this for entertainment purposes only.
