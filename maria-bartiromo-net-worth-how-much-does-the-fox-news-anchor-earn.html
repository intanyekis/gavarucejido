Maria Bartiromo Net Worth: How Much Does the Fox News Anchor Earn?

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, June 12, 2024

Maria Bartiromo is one of the most prominent and influential journalists in the American media industry. She is best known as the host of Mornings with Maria and Maria Bartiromo’s Wall Street on the Fox Business channel, and Sunday Morning Futures With Maria Bartiromo on the Fox News channel. She is also the editor of the global market at Fox Business Network. But how much does she make and what is her net worth?

Early Life and Career

Maria Bartiromo was born on September 11, 1967, in Brooklyn, New York, to Italian-American parents Vincent and Josephine Bartiromo. She attended an all-girls private Catholic school called Fontbonne Hall Academy and later graduated from New York University with a Bachelor of Arts degree in journalism and economics.

She began her career as an intern at CNN, where she worked as a producer and assignment editor for five years. She then joined CNBC in 1993, where she became the first television journalist to deliver live reports from the floor of the New York Stock Exchange. She hosted several shows on CNBC, such as Closing Bell, On the Money with Maria Bartiromo, The Business of Innovation, and Business Center.

She left CNBC in 2013 and moved to Fox Business Network and Fox News Channel, where she currently hosts three shows: Mornings with Maria, Maria Bartiromo’s Wall Street, and Sunday Morning Futures With Maria Bartiromo. She also frequently appears on other Fox News programs, such as Hannity, The Ingraham Angle, and Tucker Carlson Tonight.

Awards and Achievements

Maria Bartiromo has received numerous awards and honors for her work as a journalist and author. She has won two Emmy Awards: one for Outstanding Coverage of a Breaking News Story in 2008 and another for Outstanding Business and Economic Reporting in 2009. She has also been inducted into the Cable Hall of Fame and the National Italian American Foundation Hall of Fame.

She has written several books, including The 10 Laws of Enduring Success, The Weekend That Changed Wall Street, Use the News: How to Separate the Noise from the Investment Nuggets and Make Money in Any Economy, and The Cost: Trump, China, and American Revival. She has also been featured in several magazines, such as Forbes, Fortune, Time, Newsweek, Financial Times, and New York Magazine.

Controversies and Lawsuits

Maria Bartiromo has also faced some controversies and lawsuits during her career. In 2017, she was accused of having a cozy relationship with former President Donald Trump and his allies, often echoing their claims of election fraud and voter irregularities without providing evidence or challenging them. She was also criticized for spreading misinformation about the COVID-19 pandemic and its origins.

In 2021, she was sued by Smartmatic, a voting technology company that alleged that she defamed them by falsely accusing them of being involved in a conspiracy to rig the 2020 presidential election in favor of Joe Biden. The lawsuit sought $2.7 billion in damages from Bartiromo and other Fox News personalities. Bartiromo filed a motion to dismiss the case, arguing that she was merely reporting on newsworthy allegations made by others.

Net Worth and Salary

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Maria Bartiromo has a net worth of $50 million as of 2021. She earns an annual salary of $10 million from Fox News . She also makes money from her books, speaking engagements, endorsements, and investments.

Maria Bartiromo is one of the richest and most successful journalists in America. She has built a reputation as a hard-working and influential financial reporter and news anchor who covers some of the most important stories and events in the world. She lives a lavish lifestyle with her husband Jonathan Steinberg, who is the CEO of WisdomTree Investments, a publicly traded asset management company. They have been married since 1999 and do not have any children. They own several properties in New York City and Westhampton Beach
