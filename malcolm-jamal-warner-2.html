Who is Malcolm Jamal Warner?

Malcolm-Jamal Warner is an American rapper and actor. Malcolm-Jamal Warner (born August 18, 1970) is an American television actor, television director, and musician best known for his role as Theo Huxtable on The Cosby Show. He is also a member of the band Theo Huxtable. In addition, he starred as Malcolm McGee in the UPN sitcom Malcolm & Eddie for a brief period. Reed Between the Lines, a new BET sitcom in which he portrays Dr. Alex Reed, is presently in production.

Early Life and Bio

Malcolm-Jamal Warner was born in Jersey City, New Jersey, and was raised by his divorced mother, Pamela Warner, who also functioned as his manager. Malcolm-Jamal Warner is the son of Malcolm-Jamal Warner and Pamela Warner.

He was given the name Ahmad Jamal in honor of Malcolm X and jazz artist Ahmad Jamal. Malcolm showed an early interest in the entertainment industry when he was nine years old, which led to his participation in acting schools. His active childhood led to his graduation from The Professional Children’s School in New York City, where he received his high school diploma.

Career

In addition to guest appearances and parts on a variety of television shows and feature films, he is most known for his portrayal of Theo Huxtable, the only son of Dr. Heathcliff “Cliff” Huxtable, who was played by Bill Cosby on the NBC-TV sitcom The Cosby Show from 1984 to 1992. Warner was picked for the job by Cosby himself after auditioning for it on the very last day of a nationwide search that lasted several months.

He has also appeared in Jeremiah, provided the voice of The Producer on The Magic School Bus, and co-starred with comic Eddie Griffin on the UPN sitcom Malcolm & Eddie for four seasons. The CBS sitcom Listen Up! starring Jason Alexander, as well as the host of the literacy-promoting children’s show CBS Storybreak, allowed Warner to continue his acting career. His guest appearance on an episode of The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air in the role of Hilary’s boyfriend was his first television appearance. He has directed several music videos, including the video for British R&B group Five Star’s “I Love You For Sentimental Reasons” (1994), as well as many sitcom episodes, including The Cosby Show, All That, Kenan & Kel, and Malcolm & Eddie’s. In 1988, Warner appeared in the New Edition music video N.E Heartbreak, which was released in 1988.
