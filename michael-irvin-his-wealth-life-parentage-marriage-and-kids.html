Michael Irvin, the former NFL wide receiver, is a renowned American sports commentator. Since 2009, he has been an analyst for NFL Network. He was a student at the University of Miami and was chosen in the first round of the 1988 NFL Draft by the Dallas Cowboys.

Michael Irvin Career

The Dallas Cowboys picked Irvin as the 11th selection in the first round of the 1988 NFL Draft. He was the Cowboys’ final first-round pick under the leadership of Tex Schramm, Gil Brandt, and Tom Landry. Irvin rapidly became the first rookie wide receiver in two decades to start a season opener for the Cowboys, scoring his first career touchdown.

Irvin also caught three touchdown passes in the Cowboys’ victory over the Washington Redskins, one of the team’s only three wins that season and Landry’s last game.

Unfortunately, the following year brought more misfortune to the Cowboys, with a 1–15 record, the franchise’s worst to date. Irvin was limited to just six games after tearing the anterior cruciate ligament in his right knee against the San Francisco 49ers and subsequently being placed on the injured reserve list.

Due to injury, he didn’t play until the fourth game of the 1990 season, and his first catch didn’t come until the seventh game. By the end of the season, he had secured only 20 receptions for 413 yards, averaging 20.7 yards per catch.
